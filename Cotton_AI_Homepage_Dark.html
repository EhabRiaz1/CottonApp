The Problem

Billions in cotton. Bought on gut feel.

Textile mills make procurement decisions worth millions every year with no predictive tools, no systematic backtesting, and no integration between futures markets and physical buying.

i.

No timing framework

Mills rely on broker calls and market sentiment — not data. A single mistimed fixation on a large cargo can cost hundreds of thousands.

ii.

Origin selection by habit

Same origins year after year without analyzing which cotton-quality combinations deliver the best value for specific yarn requirements.

iii.

Hedging is an afterthought

Most mills don't hedge or hedge inconsistently. Price crashes like 2022–23 hit unprotected mills with devastating impact.
